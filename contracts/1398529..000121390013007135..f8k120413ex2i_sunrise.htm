SECURITIES PURCHASE AGREEMENT Exhibit 2.1 SHARE EXCHANGE AGREEMENT This SHARE EXCHANGE AGREEMENT (this "Agreement"), effective as of December 5, 2013, is entered into by and among Sunrise Global, Inc., a Nevada corporation (the "Parent"), Greenkraft, Inc., a California corporation (the "Company"), and George Gemayel, the sole shareholder of the Company (the "Shareholder"). Each of the parties to this Agreement are individually referred to herein as a "Party" and collectively as the "Parties." BACKGROUND A. The Company's authorized equity capital consists exclusively of 500,000,000 shares of common stock, no par value ("Company Common Stock"), of which 100,000,000 shares of Company Common Stock are issued and outstanding as of the date hereof (the "Company Shares"). George Gemayel is the record and beneficial owner of all of the Company Shares. B. The Parent's authorized equity capital consists (i) 100,000,000 shares of common stock, par value $0.001 per share ("Parent Common Stock"), of which 3,357,830 shares are issued and outstanding as of the date hereof and (ii) 100,000,000 shares of preferred stock, par value $0.001 per share, of which no shares are issued and outstanding as of the date hereof. C. Greenkraft, Inc., a Nevada corporation (the "Acquisition Sub") is a wholly- owned subsidiary of the Parent. Acquisition Sub's equity capital consists of 100,000,000 shares of common stock, par value $0.001 per share of which 100,000 shares are issued and outstanding and owned by Parent as of the date hereof and (ii) 100,000,000 shares of preferred stock, par value $0.001 per share, of which no shares are issued and outstanding as of the date hereof. D. The Shareholders wish to transfer all of their Company Shares to the Acquisition Sub in exchange for forty-one million five hundred thousand (41,500,000) ("Parent Shares") of Parent Common Stock. E. The exchange of Company Shares for Parent Common Stock is intended to constitute a non-taxable transfer to a corporation controlled by the transferor within the meaning of Section 368(a)(1)(B) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986 (the "Code"), as amended or such other tax free provisions that may be applicable under the Code. F. The Board of Directors of the Company and the Board of Directors of the Parent have determined that it is desirable and in the best interest of their respective stockholders to effect this plan of reorganization and share exchange.
